Alireza SheikhSarraf

My interest in architecture is rooted in the growing up of living in a century-old historic house in Isfahan-one of the historical cities of Iran that contains the second largest square in the world- Naghshe Jahan Square. This experience led to my deep understanding of historic fabrics, but confronting the functional challenges of these buildings focused my attention on the critical issue of Energy. I believe the responsibility of today's architect is not merely aesthetics, but efficient Resource Management. Consequently, I have focused my professional and research path on the integration of Cultural Heritage and Sustainable Architecture. My goal is to develop strategies for energy storage and preventing Energy Waste in design, thereby aligning historical identity with modern Green Architecture standards.
